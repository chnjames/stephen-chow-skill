# Rights And Safety

This file provides product guardrails, not legal advice. Laws and platform rules change; verify current requirements in the target jurisdiction before public or commercial release.

## Risk Tiers

### Green

- Factual filmography and criticism supported by sources.
- High-level discussion of comic structure, themes, genre, and cultural context.
- New work with independent characters, setting, plot, dialogue, and visual design.
- Transformation of material the user owns or is authorized to use.

### Amber

- Brief quotation for commentary, criticism, or research.
- Parody, pastiche, or comparative advertising.
- Commercial prompts using a living creator's name as a style shortcut.
- User claims of authorization without evidence when the requested output implies endorsement.

Minimize quotation, attribute it, separate fact from opinion, and recommend rights review for publication.

### Red

- Impersonating Stephen Chow or claiming he authored, approved, endorsed, or appeared in output.
- Cloning a living person's face or voice, or designing deceptive synthetic media, without documented authorization.
- Supplying full scripts, subtitles, songs, lengthy dialogue, or scene-by-scene substitutes.
- Unauthorized sequels, spin-offs, character continuations, or near-remakes.
- Removing AI labels, provenance metadata, or platform disclosures.

## Response Pattern

Use:

```text
I can't help present this as [person/official property] or reproduce that protected material. I can preserve the high-level goal by creating [new premise, characters, comic mechanism, and emotional outcome].
```

Do not overstate legal conclusions. Distinguish among identity/personality rights, copyright, trademarks, passing off/false endorsement, privacy, contract, and platform policy.

## Data Rules

- Do not bundle pirated scripts, subtitles, rips, fan transcript dumps, face datasets, or voice samples.
- Record source URL, publisher, publication date when available, access date, claim scope, and confidence.
- Prefer official laws, courts, rightsholders, archives, award bodies, and peer-reviewed research.
- Treat interviews as evidence of what was said, not proof of every underlying fact.
- Obtain permission before retaining private user scripts or unreleased business material.

## China Release Checklist

- Verify the current versions of generative-AI, deep-synthesis, personal-information, copyright, advertising, and synthetic-content-labeling rules.
- Display AI identity/disclosure where required and preserve machine-readable provenance where applicable.
- Do not use a natural person's name, likeness, or recognizable voice to imply authorization.
- Establish complaint, takedown, correction, and source-update procedures.

Use `source-registry.json` as a starting index, not as a substitute for current legal review.
