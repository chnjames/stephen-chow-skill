# Comedy Mechanism Framework

Use this vocabulary to analyze and construct comedy. These are general mechanisms, not claims about any creator's private process.

## Core Model

Comedy requires a legible reality, a violated expectation, and a consequence. Randomness without a stable rule is noise.

### Character Engines

- **Sincere underdog**: low status, high conviction, concrete want.
- **Misplaced dignity**: a person protects status in a setting that cannot support it.
- **Literal operator**: executes a metaphor, euphemism, or social convention literally.
- **False expert**: performs expertise until reality demands competence.
- **Hidden tenderness**: abrasive behavior conflicts with a caring action.

### Structural Engines

- **Status inversion**: authority and weakness exchange places through action.
- **Rule collision**: two internally valid systems meet and cannot coexist.
- **Genre displacement**: epic treatment is applied to a trivial problem, or mundane procedure to an epic event.
- **Repetition with mutation**: repeat a pattern three times; change stakes, interpretation, or cost each time.
- **Delayed consequence**: an earlier minor choice returns with disproportionate force.
- **Object callback**: a prop changes meaning across setup, escalation, and payoff.
- **Public/private split**: what a character performs publicly conflicts with what the audience can see.
- **Emotional recoil**: laughter exposes loneliness, loyalty, shame, love, or dignity.

### Beat Architecture

1. **Setup**: make place, goal, and social order clear.
2. **Rule**: establish the pattern the audience can predict.
3. **Disruption**: violate the rule for a character-driven reason.
4. **Escalation**: increase cost or status exposure; do not simply increase volume.
5. **Reversal**: reveal that the apparent winner misunderstood the game.
6. **Consequence**: make the comic action alter the situation.
7. **Emotional return**: reconnect to the protagonist's want.

## Combination Rules

- Use two primary mechanisms and at most one supporting mechanism per short scene.
- Tie every escalation to a prior choice.
- Give each major character a different interpretation of the same event.
- Prefer visible action over explanatory dialogue.
- Let the emotional beat change the meaning of an earlier joke.

## Anti-Patterns

- Catchphrases pasted onto unrelated situations.
- Humiliation with no agency, consequence, or perspective.
- References that require recognizing a specific film to work.
- A sequence of surprises without an established rule.
- Characters who exist only to deliver punchlines.
- Sentiment that arrives without prior behavioral evidence.
