# Creation Workflows

## Short Video: 15-90 Seconds

1. Express the premise in one sentence.
2. Hook with an unresolved visual contradiction in the first 10%.
3. Establish one rule and one character want.
4. Escalate twice; make the second escalation reinterpret the first.
5. Pay off a prop, gesture, or line introduced early.
6. End on consequence or emotional recoil, not explanation.

Deliver a timed beat sheet, dialogue, shot/performance notes, and caption options. Never promise engagement metrics.

## Sketch Or Short Scene: 2-8 Minutes

1. Map status and private objectives.
2. Choose a rule collision and a secondary mechanism.
3. Draft seven beats using the architecture in `style-framework.md`.
4. Ensure the midpoint changes who controls the scene.
5. Make the ending resolve the external event and alter a relationship.

## Advertisement

1. Preserve product truth and mandatory claims.
2. Make the product causally necessary; do not attach it after the joke.
3. Avoid fabricated efficacy, endorsements, reviews, or comparative claims.
4. Produce three routes: character-led, situation-led, and visual-object-led.
5. Include a plain-language claim check and AI disclosure note when relevant.

## Feature Or Series Development

1. Define theme as a conflict, not a slogan.
2. Build an original world with independent rules.
3. Give protagonist, opponent, and ally incompatible but understandable wants.
4. Track setup/payoff, status, and emotional debt by sequence.
5. Audit every major beat for resemblance to existing films before dialogue drafting.

## Rewrite And Diagnosis

Diagnose before rewriting:

```text
Symptom -> structural cause -> smallest repair -> optional bold repair
```

Preserve what the user identifies as essential. Explain changes through mechanism and character logic, not through claims that a living creator "would" make them.

## Three-Concept Diversity Test

Three concepts are materially different only if at least three of these differ:

- protagonist occupation or social position;
- setting and governing rule;
- primary mechanism;
- object or action carrying the payoff;
- relationship at stake;
- emotional ending.
