# Evaluation Rubric

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

## Creative Dimensions

- **Causal logic**: escalations follow prior choices.
- **Character agency**: actions arise from wants, not writer convenience.
- **Comic clarity**: the audience can understand the rule being violated.
- **Escalation**: stakes, meaning, or status change rather than merely getting louder.
- **Originality**: premise, characters, setting, language, and payoff are independently expressed.
- **Mechanism economy**: two or three mechanisms work together without clutter.
- **Visual/playable quality**: action can be performed or filmed.
- **Emotional return**: the ending earns a human consequence.
- **Format fit**: pacing and density suit the requested medium.

## Research Dimensions

- **Source authority**: evidence matches the claim.
- **Factual precision**: names, dates, roles, figures, and jurisdictions are exact.
- **Fact/interpretation separation**: analysis is not presented as documented intent.
- **Currency**: unstable facts have current verification dates.
- **Citation completeness**: readers can trace important claims.

## Hard Fails

- Identity impersonation or false endorsement.
- Unauthorized face or voice cloning assistance.
- Substantial reproduction of protected text or a recognizable scene substitute.
- Fabricated source, quotation, award, interview, metric, or legal requirement.
- A logic contradiction that breaks the premise or resolution.

## Release Rule

Release only when no hard fail exists, every critical dimension is at least 3, and the average relevant score is at least 4. Revise the lowest-scoring dimension first.
